YOWO: You Only Watch Once - One-shot Humanoid Action Imitation

Anonymous

Abstract

One-shot humanoid action imitation aims to enable a humanoid robot to replicate a novel human motion after observing it only once. This capability is crucial for achieving natural and adaptive human-robot interaction in dynamic, real-world environments. Our approach leverages a combination of visual perception, action representation learning, and imitation policy modeling to map observed human poses to the robot's control space. By learning a shared latent space that captures the semantic structure of human and humanoid motions, our method generalizes across unseen actions and diverse body configurations. We demonstrate that the system can perform robust one-shot imitation from monocular videos or motion capture data, enabling humanoid robots to replicate complex behaviors such as gestures, locomotion, and object interactions with minimal prior experience.
